Politics

The 1970's and 80's were rife with political action, activism and aggression. Between the 'end' of the Vietnam war, Reagan's election, and Reagan's re-election, they were politically heavy times.

Above is the speech giver by Richard Nixon declaring an end to the violence in Vietnam. What he doesn't say is that the war, and the other American experiements with violence and economics abroad, were just beginning.
This decade would mark the beginning of wide-spread, American-led, global capitalsm. With Reagenomics catching on and being spread into the SOuth American countries, the 1980's would be a decade of American influence and experimentation.

Technology

Space shuttle Challenger makes a surrealistic impression as it moves through the fog on its way down the 3.5-mile crawlerway en route to Launch Pad 39A on Nov. 30, 1982

During this decade, in the aftermath of the Space Race, came a new period of global cooperation when it came to exploring the new frontier beyond our planet. The rampant public interest in space was at an all-time high, funding was flowing and advancements were being made with an unseen rapidity. Between the launches of multiple space stations and the first deep space probes, the 1970's and 80's brought humans closer to the stars than ever before.

Thanks to NASA, we can see the effect this era of space exploration has had on the field. Take a look at some of the images that never would have been taken if it weren't for an era of enthusiasm surrounding space.

Pop Culture

The pop culture of the 1970's and 80's was counter-cultural, edgy and new. The activism of the previous decades was becoming more main-stream and seeping into media, music and journalism.
New franchises such as Star Wars and Star Trek were forward thinking and futuristic, everything way aimed at a better and brighter future. Bands such as Queen, Fleetwood Mac and The Rolling Stones were taking the world by storm with their music aimed at hippes and leftists. There was change in the culture, even if the politics didn't reflect that.

Fans gather under the marquee for Star Wars: A New Hope, the first in the Star Wars franchise, at its release in 1977.

One of the defining points of the decade, no matter who one was or where they were living, was the live aid concert of 1985. It rocked the world and acted as uniting factor, connecting people through classic rock.
